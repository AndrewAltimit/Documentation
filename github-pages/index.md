## Physics

### Classical Mechanics
**[Andrew's Notes](docs/physics/classical-mechanics.html)**

Classical Mechanics is the study of the motion of objects and the forces that cause this motion. It is based on Newton's laws of motion and covers a wide range of phenomena, from the motion of everyday objects to celestial bodies.

- [Introduction to Classical Mechanics](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/8-01sc-classical-mechanics-fall-2016/)
- [Newton's Laws of Motion](https://www.khanacademy.org/science/physics/forces-newtons-laws)
- [Conservation Laws](https://www.feynmanlectures.caltech.edu/I_04.html)
- [Dynamics and Kinematics](https://www.physicsclassroom.com/class/1DKin)

### Thermodynamics
**[Andrew's Notes](docs/physics/thermodynamics.html)**

Thermodynamics is the branch of physics that deals with heat, work, temperature, and the statistical behavior of systems with large numbers of particles. It bridges the microscopic world with macroscopic properties.

- [Laws of Thermodynamics](https://chem.libretexts.org/Bookshelves/Physical_and_Theoretical_Chemistry_Textbook_Maps/Supplemental_Modules_(Physical_and_Theoretical_Chemistry)/Thermodynamics/The_Four_Laws_of_Thermodynamics)
- [Heat Engines and Refrigerators](https://www.khanacademy.org/science/physics/thermodynamics)
- [Phase Transitions](https://www.nature.com/subjects/phase-transitions-and-critical-phenomena)
- [Chemical Thermodynamics](https://chem.libretexts.org/Bookshelves/Physical_and_Theoretical_Chemistry_Textbook_Maps/DeVoes_Thermodynamics_and_Chemistry)

### Statistical Mechanics
**[Andrew's Notes](docs/physics/statistical-mechanics.html)**

Statistical mechanics provides the microscopic foundation for thermodynamics by connecting the behavior of individual particles to macroscopic observables through statistical methods.

- [Statistical Ensembles](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/8-333-statistical-mechanics-i-statistical-mechanics-of-particles-fall-2013/)
- [Partition Functions](https://www.damtp.cam.ac.uk/user/tong/statphys.html)
- [Quantum Statistics](https://www.feynmanlectures.caltech.edu/III_04.html)
- [Phase Transitions and Critical Phenomena](https://arxiv.org/abs/0906.3454)

### Relativity
**[Andrew's Notes](docs/physics/relativity.html)**

Relativity is a branch of physics that deals with the relationship between space and time. It includes two major theories: Special Relativity and General Relativity, both formulated by Albert Einstein.

- [Special Relativity](https://www.einstein-online.info/en/elementary/)
- [General Relativity](https://arxiv.org/abs/gr-qc/9712019)
- [Spacetime](https://www.einstein-online.info/en/spotlights/)
- [Gravitational Waves](https://www.ligo.caltech.edu/page/what-are-gw)

### Quantum Mechanics
**[Andrew's Notes](docs/physics/quantum-mechanics.html)**

Quantum Mechanics is a fundamental theory in physics that describes the behavior of matter and energy at the atomic and subatomic scales. It departs from classical mechanics and introduces the concept of wave-particle duality, among other phenomena.

- [Introduction to Quantum Mechanics](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/8-04-quantum-physics-i-spring-2016/)
- [Wave-Particle Duality](https://www.feynmanlectures.caltech.edu/III_01.html)
- [Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/qt-uncertainty/)
- [Quantum Tunneling](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WPZLRtyvEqo)

### Condensed Matter Physics
**[Andrew's Notes](docs/physics/condensed-matter.html)**

Condensed matter physics studies the physical properties of matter in its condensed phases, primarily solids and liquids. It encompasses phenomena from superconductivity to topological insulators.

- [Crystal Structure and Band Theory](https://www.doitpoms.ac.uk/tlplib/brillouin_zones/)
- [Superconductivity](https://www.aps.org/programs/outreach/history/historicsites/bednorz-mueller.cfm)
- [Magnetism and Magnetic Materials](https://magnetism.eu/esm/)
- [Topological Phases of Matter](https://arxiv.org/abs/1609.05953)

### Quantum Field Theory
**[Andrew's Notes](docs/physics/quantum-field-theory.html)**

Quantum Field Theory (QFT) is the theoretical framework that combines quantum mechanics with special relativity to describe the fundamental forces and particles of nature. It serves as the foundation for the Standard Model of particle physics.

- [Introduction to Quantum Field Theory](https://www.damtp.cam.ac.uk/user/tong/qft.html)
- [The Standard Model](https://home.cern/science/physics/standard-model)
- [Feynman Diagrams](https://www.quantumdiaries.org/2010/02/14/lets-draw-feynman-diagrams/)
- [Renormalization](https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-th/0212049)

### String Theory
**[Andrew's Notes](docs/physics/string-theory.html)**

String Theory is a theoretical framework that posits that the fundamental building blocks of the universe are one-dimensional "strings" rather than point particles. It aims to provide a unified description of gravity and particle physics, potentially reconciling general relativity and quantum mechanics.

- [Introduction to String Theory](https://www.damtp.cam.ac.uk/user/tong/string.html)
- [The Basics of Strings](https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-th/0207249)
- [M-Theory](https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-th/9607201)
- [Multiverse](https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-th/0610199)
